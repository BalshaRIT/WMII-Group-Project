FreeBSD

FreeBSD was originally developed in the early 1990s as a fork of the 386BSD operating system, which was itself a derivative of BSD UNIX. FreeBSD is a popular choice for web servers, with many popular web sites, including Yahoo!, Sony, and Netflix, running on FreeBSD servers.

FreeBSD is known for its excellent documentation, which includes a comprehensive handbook and a large online community that provides support and assistance to users.

FreeBSD is released under a permissive open-source license, which allows users to modify and distribute the operating system as they see fit. FreeBSD is highly scalable and can run on a wide range of hardware architectures, including x86, ARM, MIPS, and PowerPC.

FreeBSD is designed to be highly secure, with features such as a built-in firewall, strong encryption support, and a robust access control system. FreeBSD is actively developed and maintained by a large and vibrant community of developers, who work to continually improve the operating system and add new features and capabilities.
