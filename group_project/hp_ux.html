HP-UX

HP-UX was one of the first operating systems to support logical volume management (LVM), which allowed system administrators to manage physical disk space as logical volumes.

  HP-UX was also one of the first operating systems to implement trusted computing features, such as mandatory access controls (MAC) and role-based access control (RBAC), which made it a popular choice for government and military organizations with high-security requirements.

  HP-UX supports both 32-bit and 64-bit architectures, and has been optimized to run on HP's Itanium-based Integrity servers. HP-UX has a built-in clustering technology called ServiceGuard, which allows multiple servers to work together to provide high availability and disaster recovery capabilities.

  HP-UX was one of the first operating systems to support USB devices, and also supports a wide range of other hardware devices, such as storage area networks (SANs) and tape libraries. HP-UX has been certified to comply with a number of industry standards, such as Common Criteria and FIPS, making it a popular choice for organizations with high-security and compliance requirements.
